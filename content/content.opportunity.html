Undergraduate senior projects back to top

Available

  1. Finding environment-specific genes from a large collection of metagenomics datasets.
    2.  We want to develop a data analysis pipeline for mining genes from large collection of metagenomic datasets from > 50 environments and classify environment-specific genes. Then, we will explore their functions and evolutionary history.

  2. Chromosomal recombination patterns following whole-genome duplication in fungi.
    3.  Chromosomal recombination occurs regularly during meiotic cell division and contributes to the evolution of genomes. Using chromosome conformation data of polyploid yeast that descended from genome duplications or hybridizations, we want to explore the extent that proximity influences recombination pattern.

  3. An optimization approach for generating random graphs that mimic biological networks.
    4.  We want to develop new way for generating random graphs with desired properties, and use it to study the pattern underlying some biological networks.

  4. A fast tool for detecting horizontal gene transfers in microbial genomes.
    5.  Detection of horizontal gene transfers (i.e. the event where a genomic segment from one microbial species is fused into the genome of another) usually require some prior knowledge on the two participating species. Can we develop a new method that can operate, in a timely manner of course, without that input.

    2.  We combine deep neural network with molecular simulation to predict binding affinity between a given protein and a peptide sequence.
