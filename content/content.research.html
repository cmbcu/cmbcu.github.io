Highlights of our past and current projects.

Project 1: Evolution of hybrid eukaryotic genomes

Motivation
Merging of distinct genomes into a new organism induces large-scale evolutionary processes that eventually lead to the emergence of species with novel phenotypes and capabilities -- many of which are important in agriculture and biotechnology.

Understanding of the mechanisms that drive post-hybridization genome evolution can lead to improvement in the engineering of new hybrid species (such as yeasts) that possess desirable properties.

Our approaches
Together with our collaborators in Japan, we recently discovered a group of closely related, recently formed yeast hybrids that serves as a perfect platform for investigating the early stages of post-hybridization evolution.

We are employing an integrated analysis of genome sequence, gene expression level, and chromatin structure data.

Project 2: Mass spectrometry-based de novo peptide sequencing

Motivation
Mass spectrometry technology enables high-throughput identification and quantification of peptides and proteins in a complex sample such as patient's blood and snake's venom.

However, analysis of mass spectrometry data typically requires an input database containing known peptides and proteins. In other words, we cannot identify what we did not expect.

In many applications, we simply do not know what peptides and proteins would be found and/or the possibilities are too vast to be computationally feasible.

Our approaches
We have developed deep neural network models, using millions of mass spectra, for identifying peptides without relying on protein databases. And we are still continuing to improve our peptide de novo sequencing capability even further.

In addition to method development, we are also deploying our models to identify new peptides in various biological samples and disease models.

Project 3: The distribution and community structure of microorganisms

Motivation
Microbes possess exceptional dispersability and population sizes that allow them to colonize new habitats.

Study of global microbe-environment association pattern would provide insights into the forces and constraints that shape the distribution and community structure of these organisms.

Our approaches
Our recent work suggests that generalist microbes are responsible for spreading microbial species and genes across diverse environments.

Knowledge of how microbial species and genes propagate may lead to development of better control and monitoring of future outbreaks.

Project 4: Neoantigen discovery for cancer immunotherapy

Motivation
Immunotherapy is a promising treatment for cancer, with minimal adverse effect and highly specific for each tumor.

Center of Excellence in Systems Biology at Chulalongkorn University is leading the effort to bring affordable and effective neoantigen-based immunotherapy to Thai cancer patients.

Our approaches
We develope deep learning models that aid the discovery of neoantigens expressed on the patient's tumor cell surface and prioritize neoantigens that would bind strongly to the patient's HLA proteins.

Molecular dynamics simulation will be used to assess the stability and strength of binding between neoantigen, HLA protein, and T-cell receptor.

Project 5: AI-assisted rapid chest radiograph reading

Motivation
Chest radiograph, or chest x-ray, is the most common imaging method for diagnosing diseases - including COVID-19.

Shortage of expert radiorogists means that many chest radiographs have not been not thoroughly examined.

Our approaches
Increasing availability of public chest radiograph datasets, such as the NIH Chest14, MIMIC, PadChest, and VinDr-CXR, permits development of accurate deep learning models for abnormality classification.

However, decisions that impact human life must be not only accurate but also explainable. Our group focuses on improving the explanation of deep classification model and delivering it in the way that radiologist finds useful.

Project 6: Morphological analysis of cancer cells

Motivation
Tumor cells are highly heterogeneous. Detection of tumor cells by surface protein marker only capture specific subpopulations.

Brightfield and fluorescence imaging techniques can capture and provide morphological information of the majority of cells in the samples.

Our approaches
We are working with the Excellence Center for Stem Cell and Cell Therapy at King Chulalongkorn Memorial Hospital to grow organoids of diverse cancers and use high-content imaging to study their morphology.

Machine learning and image processing techniques are used to distinguish cancer calls from normal cells, identify potential new cancer cell subtypes, and study cell transformation.

Project 7: Single-cell transcriptomics

Motivation
Single-cell technology lets us elucidate stem cell development and profile heterogeneous tumor tissue in a single experiment.

Our approaches
We are applying various bioinformatics pipelines and data visualization techniques to help our collaborators uncover cell subpopulations that could have diagnostic or prognostic values and unlock the keys to driving stem cells toward desired fates.

Systems of interest include haematopoiesis, limbal stem cell, and colorectal and hepatic tumors.

Project 8: Applications of machine learning for biomedical research

Motivation
Machine learning has become a key approach in modern research and medical service.

Research groups at Chulalongkorn University and Hospital are continually generating large amounts of data that require systematic managements and analyses.

Our approaches
We work closely with both physicians and computer engineers in order to fully understand the research goals (on the medical side) and the pros and cons of analysis techniques (on the engineering side).

Python and MATLAB are primarily used.
